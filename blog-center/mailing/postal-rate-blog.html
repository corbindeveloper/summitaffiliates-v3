    Navigating the 2026 USPS Rate Increases: How Your Business Can Continue to Save

    If your business relies on sending invoices, marketing materials, or physical packages, you are likely to feel the pinch of rising postage costs. The United States Postal Service (USPS) has announced a new round of rate increases, effective July 12, 2026. This marks another adjustment in a series of price hikes aimed at covering the agency's rising operational and transportation costs.

    While the cost of doing business is going up, your mailing expenses don't necessarily have to. As a Quadient dealer and mailing provider, we want to help you understand exactly what these changes mean for your bottom line—and more importantly, how you can leverage smart mailing technology to offset these increases and actually save money.



    Understanding the July 2026 USPS Rate Changes


    The upcoming rate adjustments will impact both market-dominant (mailing) products and competitive (shipping) services. Here is a breakdown of the most critical changes that businesses need to prepare for:


    First-Class Mail Increases

    The cost of sending standard letters is going up across the board.

    • First-Class Mail Forever Stamp: Increasing by 4 cents, from $0.78 to $0.82.
    • Metered 1-oz Letters: Increasing from $0.74 to $0.78.
    • Domestic Postcards: Increasing from $0.61 to $0.65.
    • Certified Mail Fee: Increasing from $5.30 to $5.55.

    Shipping Service Adjustments

    If you ship packages, you will also see changes. Following a temporary 8% increase implemented in April 2026, the USPS is introducing new rules for shipping services in July:

    • Dimensional (DIM) Weight Divisor Change: The DIM divisor is changing from 166 to 139, aligning USPS with other major carriers. This means large, lightweight packages may cost more to ship.
    • Ounce-Based Rating Elimination: USPS Ground Advantage is eliminating ounce-based rating for published Commercial rates.
    • New Noncompliance Fees: A $3.00 fee will be applied to parcels missing dimensions or with incorrect dimensions.

    How Mailing Solutions Protect Your Bottom Line


    When postal rates rise, sticking to traditional stamps and manual post office trips becomes an expensive liability. Upgrading to an on-site postage machine or mailing system provides immediate financial relief and long term operational efficiency.


    1. Instant Discounts on Every Letter


    The most immediate benefit of these postage meters is the automatic discount on First-Class Mail. While the retail rate for a 1-ounce stamped letter will jump to $0.82 in July, the metered rate will only be $0.78.


    By simply switching from stamps to a meter, your business instantly saves 4 cents on every single letter sent. For a company sending just 500 invoices or letters a month, that is an immediate savings of $240 a year—just on base postage, before factoring in time and labor savings.


    2. Deep Discounts on Parcel Shipping


    If your business ships packages, the savings become even more substantial. Shipping software, such as NeoShip or S.M.A.R.T., unlocks access to Commercial Base Pricing and USPS Merchant Rates.


    Users can save up to 47% on Priority Mail® shipments compared to retail Post Office rates. Furthermore, the software allows you to easily rate-shop between USPS, FedEx, and UPS from a single dashboard, ensuring you always select the most cost-effective shipping method for every package.


    3. Eliminating Over-Postage Errors


    "Guessing" the weight of a thick envelope or small package often leads to businesses applying too much postage just to be safe. Quadient meters feature built in, highly accurate scales. By weighing every piece precisely, you ensure you pay the exact postage required—never a penny more. Furthermore, preventing under-postage avoids the embarrassment and delay of mail being returned or arriving at your customer's door with "postage due."


    4. Reclaiming Wasted Time and Labor


    Sending an employee to the Post Office to stand in line, deal with ongoing traffic, buy stamps, and drop off packages is a massive drain on productivity.


    Consider the hidden costs:

    • 30 to 90 minutes spent traveling to and waiting at the Post Office.
    • $15 to $25+ per hour in administrative staff wages.
    • The opportunity cost of pulling staff away from core business tasks.

    With a mailing system, you can weigh, print, and process mail right from your desk or mailroom. You can also refill postage funds online 24/7, meaning your business never has to stop operations for a stamp run.


    Prepare Your Business Today

    The July 2026 USPS rate increase is unavoidable, but paying retail prices is optional. By partnering with Summit and implementing a mailing solution, you can offset these rate hikes, streamline your daily operations, and keep your business running efficiently.
